"How much does a mobile notary cost?" is one of the most common questions I get, and it makes total sense — nobody likes an open-ended answer when money's involved. So let's fix that. Here's exactly how mobile notary pricing works in Florida, broken down piece by piece, no vague "it depends" hand-waving.

## The Two Parts of Every Mobile Notary Bill

Mobile notary pricing has two separate components, and understanding the difference clears up almost every question people have.

**1. The notarial fee.** This is the actual cost of the notarization itself — the stamp, the seal, the legal act of witnessing your signature. Florida Statutes Chapter 117 caps this fee, and most notaries (including us) charge close to that maximum, currently $10 per signature or seal.

**2. The travel fee.** This covers the notary's time and mileage to physically get to you. Unlike the notarial fee, travel charges aren't capped by the state — they're set by the individual notary based on distance and circumstances.

Add those two together, and you've got your total. Simple enough once you see it laid out.

## What Travel Fees Actually Look Like

Travel fees generally scale with distance, and here's how it breaks down for our service area on the Treasure Coast:

| Distance | Area | Travel Fee | Per-Stamp Fee |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0–15 miles | Vero Beach | $35 | $10 |
| 15–30 miles | Sebastian, Fort Pierce | $55 | $10 |
| 30–60 miles | Melbourne, Stuart, Jupiter | $75+ | $10 |

So if you're in Vero Beach and need two documents notarized, you're looking at roughly $35 travel plus $20 in notarial fees ($10 per stamp) — $55 total, give or take. Simple math, no surprise line items.

## What Bumps the Price Up

A few situations typically carry an additional charge, usually in the $25–$50 range:

- **"Emergency" requests** — anything booked with under two hours notice
- **Hospital or nursing home visits** — these usually take longer due to check-in procedures, visitor logs, and finding the right room
- **After-hours appointments** — anything after 8:00 PM

None of this is designed to nickel-and-dime you — it reflects the actual time and flexibility those situations require. A same-day hospital visit at 9 PM is simply a different job than a scheduled weekday appointment.

## Why the Per-Stamp Fee Is the Same Everywhere

Because it's set by state law, not by the notary. Whether you go to a bank, a shipping store, or book a mobile visit, the actual notarial fee is going to be close to that same $10 cap. Nobody's marking that part up — it's regulated. The only real variable across providers is the travel fee, which is where mobile notaries earn for the convenience they provide.
