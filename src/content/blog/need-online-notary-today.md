If you're reading this because something needs to be notarized *today* and you're not sure how that's even possible without a stressful scramble — good news, it usually is possible, and it's simpler than it feels right now.

## Step One: Confirm Your Document Qualifies for RON

Most everyday notarizations work fine remotely — affidavits, powers of attorney (with witnesses joining the same call), business agreements, travel consent forms, and plenty more. A few things, like certain wills or documents with strict wet-signature requirements from the receiving institution, don't. If you're not sure, a quick message describing your document gets a fast answer.

## Step Two: Gather What You Actually Need

The list is shorter than people expect:

- **The unsigned document** — don't sign it beforehand, Florida law requires signing in the notary's presence (virtual presence counts for RON)
- **A valid, government-issued photo ID** — driver's license, state ID, or passport
- **A device with a working camera and microphone** — laptop, tablet, or smartphone
- **A stable internet connection**

That's genuinely everything.

## Step Three: Reach Out Directly

For anything same-day, texting beats emailing or filling out a general contact form — it gets seen and answered faster. Include what document you need notarized and your ideal timing, and you'll get a much quicker answer than a generic inquiry would.

## Why RON Is Often Faster Than a Same-Day Mobile Visit

If you're weighing a same-day mobile notary against a same-day RON session, RON usually wins on speed. There's no travel time to coordinate, no traffic to account for, no need to find a mutually convenient physical location. A same-day RON session can sometimes happen within the hour, depending on current availability — something that's much harder to promise for an in-person visit across a service area.

## What Happens During the Session Itself

Once you're booked:

1. **You upload your document** (if you haven't already)
2. **You complete identity verification** — an ID scan plus a few knowledge-based questions
3. **You join the video session** with your notary
4. **You sign electronically**, with the notary witnessing
5. **You receive your notarized document**, typically within minutes of finishing

Most single-document sessions wrap up in 15-20 minutes total, including the identity verification step.

## If Your Document Doesn't Qualify for RON

Not every same-day need fits into a remote session. If your document specifically requires an in-person signature, or involves complexities RON doesn't cover, a same-day [mobile notary](/mobile-notary) visit may still be possible, depending on your location and current availability. Reach out with the details either way, and the fastest realistic option gets sorted out from there.
