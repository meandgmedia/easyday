You're at the gate, boarding pass in hand, and you suddenly remember a document needed to be notarized before this trip. Or worse — someone back home just told you it needs to happen *today*. Is there a notary somewhere in this airport? Almost certainly not, but here's what actually works instead.

## The Short Answer: Airport Notaries Are Rare

Unlike a bank branch or shipping store, airports generally don't have notary services available on a reliable, walk-up basis. A handful of major international airports have occasionally offered some form of notary access, often tied to a business center or currency exchange counter, but this isn't standard, isn't guaranteed, and definitely isn't something to count on at most regional or smaller airports.

If your plan was "I'll just find a notary at the airport," it's worth having a backup plan before you're already there.

## The Better Option: Remote Online Notary

This is exactly the kind of situation Remote Online Notarization solves well. Since the entire process happens over live video with no physical location requirement, you can complete a notarization from:

- **Your gate**, if you have decent Wi-Fi and enough time before boarding
- **A quiet corner of the terminal**, away from noise
- **Your hotel room**, before or after a flight
- **Literally anywhere with a device, camera, and internet connection**

## Planning Ahead vs. Scrambling Last-Minute

The best version of this situation is realizing you need something notarized *before* you're at the airport, giving yourself time to book a RON session from home, or at least from somewhere with reliable internet and a bit of breathing room. That said, if you're already traveling and just realized you need this, a RON session is still very possibly your fastest path — booking ahead just removes the added stress of doing it under a boarding announcement.

## What You'll Need, Wherever You End Up Doing It

- **A valid, government-issued photo ID**
- **The unsigned document**
- **A device with a working camera and microphone**
- **A stable internet connection** — airport Wi-Fi can be hit or miss, so a personal hotspot is worth having as backup

## Layovers: Technically Possible, Not a Reliable Plan

If you're stuck between flights with a genuinely long layover and a real internet connection, a RON session could theoretically fit into that window. But layovers are unpredictable — delays happen, gates change, connections get tight — so this isn't something to bank on as your primary plan. If you know ahead of time that you'll need something notarized around travel, book it before you leave whenever possible.
