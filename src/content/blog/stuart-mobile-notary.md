## Why Stuart and Martin County Keep Notaries Busy

- **A significant retiree population.** Estate planning documents — powers of attorney, healthcare directives, trusts — are a steady need throughout the area.
- **An active waterfront real estate market.** Stuart's marina district and riverfront properties keep closing-related notarizations common.
- **Seasonal residents.** Snowbirds handling paperwork before heading north or south for the season are a regular part of the Martin County mobile notary picture.
- **A close-knit small business community**, particularly around historic downtown Stuart.

## Documents Commonly Notarized in Stuart

- Powers of attorney and healthcare surrogate designations
- Real estate deeds and closing documents
- Trust and estate planning documents
- Affidavits and sworn statements
- Business agreements and contracts

Our guide on [medical vs financial power of attorney](/blog/medical-vs-financial-power-of-attorney) is a common starting point for Martin County residents working through estate planning.

## Serving Martin County's Retiree Community

Given how many Martin County residents are actively managing estate plans, powers of attorney and healthcare directives make up a meaningful share of the mobile notary work here. If a document has been sitting on the to-do list, getting it notarized while everything is calm and straightforward is always easier than handling it during a health crisis.
