## What "Treasure Coast" Actually Covers

For mobile notary purposes, the Treasure Coast stretches across Indian River, St. Lucie, and Martin Counties — with coverage extending a bit further into Brevard and Palm Beach Counties too. In practice, that means service throughout:

- **Vero Beach** — home base, and the center of our fastest response times
- **Sebastian** — a short drive north
- **Fort Pierce** — St. Lucie County's coastal hub
- **Port St. Lucie** — one of the fastest-growing cities on the coast, and a frequent stop for closing signings
- **Stuart** — Martin County, a little further south
- **Jensen Beach, Palm City, and Hobe Sound** — smaller communities in between, all within reach

For anyone further out — Melbourne to the north, Jupiter to the south — mobile visits are still available, just with a slightly higher travel fee based on distance.

## How Pricing Works by Area

Travel fees scale with distance from Vero Beach, broken into simple tiers:

| Distance | Cities | Travel Fee |
|---|---|---|
| 0–15 miles | Vero Beach | $35 |
| 15–30 miles | Sebastian, Fort Pierce | $55 |
| 30–60 miles | Melbourne, Stuart, Jupiter | $75+ |
| 60+ miles | Further out | Quoted individually |

Every tier includes the standard $10-per-stamp notarial fee on top. For the full breakdown, including what bumps pricing up for urgent or after-hours requests, check out our [mobile notary pricing guide](/blog/florida-mobile-notary-pricing).

## Why Local Coverage Actually Matters

A notary based two counties away sounds fine on paper, until you're the one waiting an extra hour for them to arrive — or paying a travel fee that reflects that extra distance. Being based in Vero Beach means faster response times for the core Treasure Coast area, and same-day availability that's realistic, not just advertised.

It also means a level of local familiarity that's genuinely useful: knowing which hospitals have specific visitor sign-in procedures, which title companies in Port St. Lucie tend to have tight closing windows, and how traffic on US-1 affects an afternoon schedule. Small things, but they add up to smoother appointments.

## Common Reasons Treasure Coast Residents Book a Mobile Notary

- **Real estate closings** — Port St. Lucie and Fort Pierce see a steady stream of home purchases and refinances
- **Hospital and hospice visits** — Cleveland Clinic Indian River, Lawnwood Regional, and other area facilities
- **Snowbird season paperwork** — seasonal residents needing documents notarized before heading north or south
- **Estate planning appointments** — powers of attorney and trust documents for the area's many retirees
- **Business documents** — LLC agreements and contracts for the Treasure Coast's growing small business scene
